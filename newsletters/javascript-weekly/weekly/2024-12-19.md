## 🗓️ What Happened to JavaScript in 2024

The JavaScript world has had a busy 2024, as you'd expect for [the world's most used programming language](https://www.infoworld.com/article/3625652/javascript-is-still-number-one-jetbrains-report.html) (despite the threat of [being split into two](https://devclass.com/2024/10/22/should-javascript-be-split-into-two-languages-new-google-driven-proposal-divides-opinion/)). We looked back over the year and remembered some things that occurred:

- In February, the React team posted [a mega 'React Labs' update](https://react.dev/blog/2024/02/15/react-labs-what-we-have-been-working-on-february-2024) which framed the whole of React's year, explaining the goals of the React Compiler and the eventual React 19 release (which [went stable](https://react.dev/blog/2024/12/05/react-19) this month).
- Alternative JavaScript runtimes had a great 2024, particularly Bun which introduced [Bun Shell](https://bun.sh/blog/the-bun-shell), [Windows support](https://bun.sh/blog/bun-v1.1) and the ability to [compile and run native C from JavaScript.](https://bun.sh/blog/compile-and-run-c-in-js) Deno had a big year too, with [the release of Deno 2](https://deno.com/blog/v2.0) which included stronger Node.js/npm compatibility and package management tooling. Other systems continuing to progress this year include [Boa JS](https://boajs.dev/), [QuickJS](https://bellard.org/quickjs/), and [Porffor](https://porffor.dev/).
- Over at TC39, [lots of language proposals were advanced](https://socket.dev/blog/tc39-advances-10-ecmascript-proposals-key-features-to-watch) this year (and that's just a sample – [here's some more!](https://socket.dev/blog/tc39-advances-key-proposals)). The ES2025 future is bright.

[Boost Semantic Search with MongoDB Atlas Vector Search](https://fnf.dev/3VxPgLJ) — Create embeddings, index them, and run semantic queries—just follow the quick start tutorial.

MongoDB sponsor

- Deno decided to take the JavaScript trademark fight to Oracle and [formally filed a petition with the USPTO to cancel it.](https://deno.com/blog/deno-v-oracle) We hope to see some developments here in 2025, but Oracle are prepared to defend it.
- Not content to merely work on an edge platform, JS runtime, and fight Oracle, Deno also [unveiled JSR](https://deno.com/blog/jsr-is-not-another-package-manager), a fresh attempt at providing a registry for JavaScript packages.
- There were lots of big releases generally for major JavaScript projects including [Svelte v5](https://svelte.dev/blog/svelte-5-is-alive), [Node.js v23.0](https://nodejs.org/en/blog/release/v23.0.0), [Astro 5.0](https://astro.build/blog/astro-5/), [TypeScript 5.7](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/typescript/announcing-typescript-5-7/), [Vite 6.0](https://vite.dev/blog/announcing-vite6.html), [React Native 0.76](https://reactnative.dev/blog/2024/10/23/release-0.76-new-architecture), [Next.js 15](https://nextjs.org/blog/next-15), [React Router v7](https://remix.run/blog/react-router-v7), [Rspack 1.0](https://rspack.dev/blog/announcing-1-0), [Vue.js 3.5](https://blog.vuejs.org/posts/vue-3-5), and [Angular 19](https://blog.angular.dev/meet-angular-v19-7b29dfd05b84).
- The OG of JavaScript libraries [jQuery 4.0 went into beta](https://blog.jquery.com/2024/07/17/second-beta-of-jquery-4-0-0/) too! We keep our fingers crossed for a jQuery 4.0 final release in 2025.. ;-)

## 🥇 Our Top Items of 2024

Next up is a walk through the top items we included in 2024, ordered by level of reader engagement. No editorial judgments here – these are the things you cared about most:

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/cpress/image/upload/w_1280,e_sharpen:60,q_auto/cybf82tlhtuvrkkvw5xp.jpg)

1. [`console.delight`](https://frontendmasters.com/blog/console-delight/) — *By far* our most popular link of the year with over 20,000 clicks(!) – but who doesn't use and love `console.log`? This post showed us how in the browser console it's not merely for printing plain text, but can be used to render things like SVGs and HTML.

Zach Saucier

2. [JavaScript Visualized: Promise Execution](https://www.lydiahallie.com/blog/promise-execution) — A well-diagrammed article coupled with an (optional) 8 minute video that went into how promises work under the hood. Hugely popular as most of Lydia's content tends to be.

Lydia Hallie

[WorkOS: Sell to Enterprises with a Few Lines of Code](https://workos.com/?utm_source=cpjs&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=q42024) — The modern identity platform for B2B SaaS, offering flexible, easy-to-use APIs to integrate SSO, SCIM, and FGA in minutes instead of months.

WorkOS sponsor

3. [Is htmx Just Another JavaScript Framework?](https://htmx.org/essays/is-htmx-another-javascript-framework/) — Despite being five years old, [htmx](https://htmx.org/) has seen a surge in popularity in 2023 and 2024, partly due to framework fatigue, but also as its simple HTML-oriented approach to adding functionality to pages appeals to a diverse group of developers. [v2.0](https://htmx.org/posts/2024-06-17-htmx-2-0-0-is-released/) landed in June.

Alexander Petros

[Ecma International Approves ECMAScript 2024: What’s New?](https://2ality.com/2024/06/ecmascript-2024.html) — In June, the Ecma General Assembly approved the latest ECMAScript / JavaScript spec, officially making it a standard. As with ES2023, it was a reasonably small step forward, but Dr. Axel rounded up what was new.

Dr. Axel Rauschmayer

5. [JavaScript's `??=` Operator: Default Values Made Simple](https://www.trevorlasn.com/blog/javascript-nullish-coalescing-assignment-operator) — The `??=` nullish coalescing assignment operator snuck into JavaScript a few years ago via ES2021 and has been [broadly supported](https://caniuse.com/mdn-javascript_operators_nullish_coalescing_assignment) almost everywhere for ages. Trevor showed off how it can tighten up your assignments.

Trevor I. Lasn

6. [Eloquent JavaScript: The Fourth Edition](http://eloquentjavascript.net/) — Coming several years after the third edition, the latest version of what is, perhaps, the best ‘all rounder’ book for learning JavaScript arrived in March *“adjusted to the realities of 2024 and generally touched up.”*

Marijn Haverbeke

7.
