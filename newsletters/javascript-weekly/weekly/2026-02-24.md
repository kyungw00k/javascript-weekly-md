[Goodbye `innerHTML`, Hello `setHTML` for Stronger XSS Protection](https://hacks.mozilla.org/2026/02/goodbye-innerhtml-hello-sethtml-stronger-xss-protection-in-firefox-148/) — As of v148, Firefox [supports](https://caniuse.com/mdn-api_sanitizer) the [Sanitizer API.](https://wicg.github.io/sanitizer-api/) Rather than assigning HTML to DOM nodes with `innerHTML`, you can use [`setHTML`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Element/setHTML) for safety by default. It’s a cutting-edge feature, with Chrome 146 (beta) also adding support, but nothing in Safari yet, so [DOMPurify](https://github.com/cure53/DOMPurify) will remain useful for a while.
