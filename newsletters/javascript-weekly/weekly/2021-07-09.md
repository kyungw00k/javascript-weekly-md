[The State of Web Workers in 2021](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2021/06/web-workers-2021/) — The web is typically single-threaded, but two years ago Surma told us [why to always use Web Workers](https://surma.dev/things/when-workers/) to bring multithreading to your solutions despite the difficulties. [Almost every client supports](https://caniuse.com/webworkers) them now, so Surma makes the case again and explains why [Web Workers](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Web_Workers_API/Using_web_workers) deserve a place in your projects.

Off down the `export` rabbit hole we go...

[`npm audit`: Broken by Design](https://overreacted.io/npm-audit-broken-by-design/) — *“The way `npm audit` works is broken. [Elementary: React-Inspired Functional, Declarative Audio Applications](https://www.nickwritesablog.com/functional-declarative-audio-applications/) — Can writing audio [DSP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_signal_processing) software be “*simple, fast and fun*”? Not up until now, but [Elementary](https://www.elementary.audio) intends to change all that, and thus lowering the barriers to entry for this burgeoning field.
