[Astro 3.0 Released: The All-in-One Web Framework](https://astro.build/blog/astro-3/) — An increasingly popular, turbo-charged site generator comfortable with not just static sites, but dynamic and interactive ones too by way of its [‘islands’ approach.](https://docs.astro.build/en/concepts/islands/) You can use React, Vue, Svelte, Solid, and others, with it, and v3 adds View Transitions API support ([more on that here](https://developer.chrome.com/blog/astro-view-transitions/)) which can result in striking creations like [this Spotify 'clone'](https://spotify-astro-transitions.vercel.app/). It requires a play to really 'get it' but it's great.

Astro Team

Good news – now [SvelteKit supports the View Transitions API](https://svelte.dev/blog/view-transitions) too 😉

[Node.js 20.6 Released with Built-In Support for `.env` Files](https://philna.sh/blog/2023/09/05/nodejs-supports-dotenv/) — The [official release post](https://nodejs.org/en/blog/release/v20.6.0) covers everything new in 20.6, but the highlight is support for `.env` environment files to set environment variables dynamically when a script is run without requiring a third-party library like `dotenv`.

Phil Nash

[Bye to *Rome* and Hi to *Biome* for JS Formatting and Linting](https://biomejs.dev/blog/annoucing-biome) — First announced in 2020, Rome is/was an ambitious effort to create a unified frontend dev tool to bring ideas from Babel, ESLint, webpack, etc. into one place. After forming a company around Rome and raising money two years ago, things seem to have gone awry, with its key maintainers forking the project as [*Biome.*](https://biomejs.dev/)

Emanuele Stoppa and the Biome Team

**⚡️ IN BRIEF:**

- Version 1.0 of [Bun](https://bun.sh/), the notably fast *JavaScriptCore*-based JS runtime, is due to launch in the coming hours. There's a livestream and, we assume, a launch post due to [land here very soon.](https://bun.sh/1.0)
- In other 'alternative runtime news', the Deno team has announced [native npm support on its *Deno Deploy* platform.](https://deno.com/blog/npm-on-deno-deploy) They've also announced [*Deno KV* is in open beta.](https://deno.com/blog/kv-open-beta)
- **Mozilla:** [*"Over the course of the year Firefox has improved by around 40% on the Vue.js benchmark"*](https://hacks.mozilla.org/2023/09/faster-vue-js-execution-in-firefox/) – find out why.
- David Heinemeier Hansson, the creator of *Ruby on Rails*, [announced that they're dropping TypeScript](https://world.hey.com/dhh/turbo-8-is-dropping-typescript-70165c01) from *Turbo* (a library commonly used with Rails) in favor of plain old JavaScript. Social media drama ensued.

- GitHub has made [changes to its homepage feeds.](https://github.blog/changelog/2023-09-06-updates-to-your-github-feed/) Quite a few people [are unhappy](https://github.com/orgs/community/discussions/66133) about it, though.
- [*"The CSS-in-JS sector has plateaued,"*](https://2023.stateofcss.com/en-US/css-in-js/) says the latest *State of CSS* survey.
