**⚡️ IN BRIEF:**

- Ecma International has [approved the ECMAScript 2023 spec](https://www.ecma-international.org/news/ecma-international-approves-new-standards-at-the-125th-general-assembly-27-june-2023/) (as well a standard for 1 terabyte holographic disks - neat).
- /r/javascript is one of Reddit's biggest subreddits with over 2 million followers, but it went read-only in protest of recent Reddit policies. They're now [discussing whether it should come back](https://www.reddit.com/r/javascript/comments/14i4s84/where_does_rjavascript_go_from_here/) – it's not looking likely.
- TypeScript educator Matt Pocock [🐦 says the conversation has moved on](https://twitter.com/mattpocockuk/status/1673616900461404160) from *"Should we adopt TypeScript?"* to *"How do we best use it?"*

**RELEASES:**

- [Ember.js 5.0](https://blog.emberjs.com/ember-5-0-released/) – A framework that pre-dates React, is used all over the place, but that barely anyone seems to talk about (and they *should*).
- [styled-components 6.0](https://github.com/styled-components/styled-components/releases/tag/v6.0.0) – Popular CSS styling approach for React components. There's a [migration guide](https://styled-components.com/docs/faqs#what-do-i-need-to-do-to-migrate-to-v6) for upgraders.
- [Remix 1.18.0](https://github.com/remix-run/remix/releases/tag/remix%401.18.0) – The full stack framework gains big perf improvements and stabilizes its HMR/HDR-ready "new dev server" ([explained](https://github.com/remix-run/remix/releases/tag/remix%401.14.0)).
- [esbuild 0.18.10](https://github.com/evanw/esbuild/releases) – It's had several noteworthy enhancements in the past week so we're linking to the page covering them all.
