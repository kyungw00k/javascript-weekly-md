## 🛠 Code & Tools

[Defuddle: Extract the Main Content from Web Pages](https://github.com/kepano/defuddle) — Strips extraneous clutter from HTML in order to leave only the primary content for you to format or use as you wish. Essentially a modern implementation of the ideas behind Mozilla’s [Readability.](https://github.com/mozilla/readability) There’s [an online demo](https://kepano.github.io/defuddle/) if you want to give it a try.

Steph Ango

[snapDOM: Captures DOM Nodes as Images](https://github.com/zumerlab/snapdom) — A fast and accurate DOM-to-image capture mechanism to capture any HTML element as a scalable SVG image, preserving styles, fonts, background images, etc.

CodeRabbit sponsor

[ForesightJS: Predictive Mouse Intent Library](https://foresightjs.com/) — An interesting concept, with the entire page acting as a demo of sorts. The idea is to decrease latency by prefetching data or pages as soon as you *think* a user might be heading in that direction. Your mileage may vary and it’s less useful for touch devices.

ForesightJS, Inc.

## 👀 Elsewhere..

Here's a selection of things from the broader ecosystem this week:

- Google's [I/O event](https://io.google/2025/) took place this week and [▶️ its 'developer keynote'](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GjvgtwSOCao) was a highlight, particularly when Addy Osmani showed off some of the latest Chrome DevTools fun *(starting at about 30 minutes in).*
- Microsoft has unveiled [a new Postgres extension for VS Code](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/adforpostgresql/announcing-a-new-ide-for-postgresql-in-vs-code-from-microsoft/4414648) that brings IntelliSense completion to SQL, GitHub Copilot integration, a visual way to explore your schemas, and more.
- 🤖 Anthropic has [unveiled its new Claude 4 models](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-4). The Claude family of LLMs are quite heavily used by modern coding agents, as well as Claude's own now-generally-available [Claude Code](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-code) tool.
- The *Glitch* platform, a popular way to build and host Node-backed webapps, is [shutting down its app hosting functionality](https://blog.glitch.com/post/changes-are-coming-to-glitch/) – it seems uncertain quite what Glitch will or won't be after July.
