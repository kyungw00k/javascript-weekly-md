[GitHub Unveils Its 'Super Linter': One Linter to Rule Them All?](https://github.blog/2020-06-18-introducing-github-super-linter-one-linter-to-rule-them-all/) — Setting up the right linters for the various types of code in your projects can be… a bit of a pain. **⚡️ Quick bytes:**

- [OpenHive.JS](https://anchor.fm/openhivejs) is a new JavaScript podcast from James Snell and Matteo Collina – three episodes so far including [an interview with Laurie Barth](https://anchor.fm/openhivejs/episodes/Laurie-Barth-on-Maintaining-Gatsby-ef6p8i) on maintaining Gatsby.
- The [first alpha release of Bootstrap 5](https://blog.getbootstrap.com/2020/06/16/bootstrap-5-alpha/) is out and notably drops jQuery as a dependency in favor of vanilla JS.
- First proposed *5 years ago*, [variadic tuple types are coming](https://github.com/microsoft/TypeScript/issues/5453#issuecomment-644984977) to TypeScript in TypeScript 4.0. [Examples here](https://github.com/microsoft/TypeScript/pull/39094).
- The V8 project has started a [V8 Research Grant](https://v8.dev/grant) to give up to $40k to *academic* programming language and research projects based around the V8 JavaScript engine.
- What caused TypeScript to take off? [Google announcing they were going to use it with Angular](https://www.zdnet.com/article/microsoft-lead-engineer-programming-language-typescript-took-off-thanks-to-googles-angular/), claims Ryan Cavanaugh, Microsoft's engineering lead for TypeScript.
- ThoughtWorks has written about ['Node Overload'](https://www.thoughtworks.com/radar/platforms?blipid=202005026) and asserts *"performance is no longer a reason to choose Node.js."*
