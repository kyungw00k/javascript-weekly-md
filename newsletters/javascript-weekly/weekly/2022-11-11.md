## Top Stories

[Rome v10: Rust-Powered JS Linting and Formatting](https://rome.tools/blog/2022/11/08/rome-10/) — A project founded by the [original creator of Babel](https://podcast.babeljs.io/rome/) naturally provokes interest. Rome’s ambitious mission is to unify all the frontend dev tools you’d need into one, and formatting and linting is where they’ve started with this curiously numbered release. *Shouldn't it should be Rome X? *😁

The Rome Team

**Editorial note:** *"Oh no, another tool!"* I hear you cry. Despite its youth, Rome already has some compelling points in its favor, including [rich diagnostics](https://rome.tools/blog/2022/11/08/rome-10/#rich-and-comprehensible-diagnostics) that not only drill into *what* is wrong with your code, but *why* it's wrong. It will be fantastic to see this sort of tooling advance further.

[Why Would Anyone Need Generator Functions?](https://jrsinclair.com/articles/2022/why-would-anyone-need-javascript-generator-functions/) — You can go a long time without feeling the need for [generators.](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Reference/Statements/function*) So, you may wonder, what are they good for? While they may not be *essential,* they *do* have utility and can change how you approach certain problems. James cracks out the chocolate biscuits to explain.

James Sinclair

[Gatsby 5.0: The Fastest Gatsby Yet](https://www.gatsbyjs.com/blog/gatsby-5/) — The performance oriented React-based framework (that *isn’t* Next.js) takes a leap forward with the [Slice API](https://www.gatsbyjs.com/docs/reference/built-in-components/gatsby-slice/) for speeding up common updates across a site, partial hydration (beta), a new `Script` component for loading scripts, incremental builds and deploys, and more. This release follows Gatsby’s fresh pitch as a [‘reactive site generator’](https://www.gatsbyjs.com/blog/re-introducing-gatsby-a-reactive-site-generator/).

Josh Johnson (Gatsby Team)
