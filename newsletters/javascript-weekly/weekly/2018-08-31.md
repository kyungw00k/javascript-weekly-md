[Babel 7.0 Released: The JavaScript Transpiler](https://babeljs.io/blog/2018/08/27/7.0.0) — A significant release for the popular JavaScript compiler. Some links to help you navigate your way:

- If you need just *one* reason to upgrade, 7.0 is [a lot faster.](https://twitter.com/left_pad/status/927554660508028929)
- There's a new [upgrade tool](https://github.com/babel/babel-upgrade) to help you migrate.
- [TypeScript support is now baked in.](https://blogs.msdn.microsoft.com/typescript/2018/08/27/typescript-and-babel-7/)
- [Stage presets were removed.](https://babeljs.io/blog/2018/07/27/removing-babels-stage-presets) Here's why and how to deal with it.
- [Planning for 7.0](https://babeljs.io/blog/2017/09/12/planning-for-7.0) and [Nearing the 7.0 Release](https://babeljs.io/blog/2017/12/27/nearing-the-7.0-release) will bring you up to date with what it's taken to get this far.

[Lerna Added (Then Removed) A License Term Blocking Some Companies From Using It](https://github.com/lerna/lerna/pull/1616) — If you’re not on social media, you may have missed this story which has since concluded. [Lerna](https://github.com/lerna/lerna), a popular tool for working with monorepos, relicensed itself to block its use at a variety of companies (including Microsoft) but after a lot of discussion and community feedback, [this was reverted.](https://github.com/lerna/lerna/pull/1633)
