[Tesseract.js 2.0: Pure JavaScript OCR for 100 Languages](https://tesseract.projectnaptha.com/) — A pure JavaScript port of the popular C++ [Tesseract](https://github.com/tesseract-ocr/tesseract) library commonly used for visual text recognition purposes by other systems. The homepage has a neat demo where you can drop on images of your own and see how they get processed. v2.0 is now out and [here's why the creator has been working on it.](https://t.co/ArSyMg02kS?amp=1)

[V8 Release v8.0](https://v8.dev/blog/v8-release-80) — Yes, that’s v8 of v8 – not confusing at all 😂 Nonetheless, it’s a key step forward for the most widely deployed JavaScript engine, and introduces optional chaining, nullish coalescing, and some significant performance improvements. It’ll be landing in a Chrome and Node near you soon.

Leszek Swirski

[Results from the 'State of JavaScript' 2019 Survey](https://2019.stateofjs.com/) — While the methodology is far from *perfect* (for example, Angular and Angular.js seem to get lumped together as the same thing), this is nonetheless the biggest JavaScript-specific survey. There’s a lot to dig through, so you may prefer *The Changelog's* **[7 insights from the State of JS 2019 roundup](https://changelog.com/posts/7-insights-from-the-state-of-js-2019)** post that looks at the most striking results. I also found the associated [Hacker News discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=21831747) interesting.

Sacha Greif, Raphaël Benitte and Amelia Wattenberger

[An Update on CDNJS](https://blog.cloudflare.com/an-update-on-cdnjs/) — [CDNJS](https://cdnjs.com/) is a popular content distribution network for JavaScript files but just a couple of months ago [its continued development was in doubt.](https://github.com/cdnjs/cdnjs/issues/13524) In this significant update we learn more about how CDNJS works and how Cloudflare want to take it forward (and they need our help).

Zack Bloom

[Mastering `console.log` Like A Pro](https://medium.com/javascript-in-plain-english/mastering-js-console-log-like-a-pro-1c634e6393f9) — This isn’t exactly *new* ground, but if you’re a heavy `console.log` user, this article deftly covers a variety of extra console methods to keep on your radar.

Harsh Makadia
