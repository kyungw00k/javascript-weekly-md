## 🗞 JavaScript developments in 2018

- [jQuery 3.3 was released](http://blog.jquery.com/2018/01/19/jquery-3-3-0-a-fragrant-bouquet-of-deprecations-and-is-that-a-new-feature/), much to the relief of the majority who love jQuery but pretend they don't.
- TC39 continued to get and work upon [lots of proposals](https://github.com/tc39/proposals) on how to improve the language.
- Lin Clark helped everyone [understand ES modules](https://hacks.mozilla.org/2018/03/es-modules-a-cartoon-deep-dive/), which all major browsers (finally) [came to support](https://caniuse.com/#feat=es6-module) in 2018.
- Martin Fowler announced he was switching his famous *Refactoring* book from Java to JavaScript for [its long-awaited second edition.](https://martinfowler.com/articles/refactoring-2nd-ed.html)
- [webpack 4.0](https://medium.com/webpack/webpack-4-released-today-6cdb994702d4) came out, getting a *lot* faster and adopting an optional 'zero config' approach as favored by competing tools like [Parcel](https://parceljs.org/).

- We found out that [Microsoft has been rewriting Office 365 in JavaScript.](https://twitter.com/TheLarkInn/status/1006746626617008128)
- ECMA published [the official ECMAScript 2018 specification](https://www.ecma-international.org/publications/standards/Ecma-262.htm), thus ensuring all JavaScript developers had plentiful bedtime reading all summer. Dr. Axel can help if you [want to understand it.](http://exploringjs.com/es2018-es2019/toc.html)
- [Node 10 and npm 6 came out.](https://nodeweekly.com/issues/235)
- Anil Dash asked what would happen if JavaScript [became the first-ever truly dominant programming language?](https://medium.com/@anildash/what-if-javascript-wins-84898e5341a)
- Redux 'died', then [came back to life](https://blog.isquaredsoftware.com/2018/03/redux-not-dead-yet/), [died again](https://hackernoon.com/goodbye-redux-26e6a27b3a0b), then [back to life](https://github.com/reduxjs/react-redux/releases/tag/v6.0.0) again.
- Oracle's ownership of the 'JavaScript' trademark continued to annoy developers, even prompting [a suggestion to rename the language.](https://www.techrepublic.com/article/why-its-finally-time-to-give-up-on-the-name-javascript/)
- 2 years of hard work translated into [Babel 7](https://babeljs.io/blog/2018/08/27/7.0.0) being released.
- Microsoft [bought GitHub](https://news.microsoft.com/2018/06/04/microsoft-to-acquire-github-for-7-5-billion/) for $7.5bn, then they [removed jQuery from their frontend](https://githubengineering.com/removing-jquery-from-github-frontend/). They haven't moved to Azure as yet.
- [JSConf US](https://2018.jsconf.us/) came back to life after a hiatus.
- The annual [State of JavaScript](https://2018.stateofjs.com/) survey ran, got over 20,000 responses, and told us React continues to flourish, everyone wants to learn Vue.js, and the use of ES6 is now very well established. Also, [everyone's using VS Code now.](https://2018.stateofjs.com/other-tools/)
- Evan You gave us a [preview of Vue.js 3.0.](https://medium.com/vue-mastery/evan-you-previews-vue-js-3-0-ab063dec3547)
- The V8 JavaScript engine [turned 10 years old.](https://v8.dev/blog/10-years) The work on V8 over the years has been instrumental in making JavaScript as popular as it is now, IMHO.
- The React team unveiled [Hooks](https://reactjs.org/docs/hooks-intro.html), an experimental feature in React 16.7 that got a lot of React developers very excited and seems as if it may become a key element of React development in 2019.
- A [popular npm package was exploited](https://schneid.io/blog/event-stream-vulnerability-explained/) causing a lot of discussion about how we prevent such things in future.
- Marijn Haverbeke spent the whole year writing [a third edition of Eloquent JavaScript.](https://eloquentjavascript.net/) It's almost finished.
- Lots of versions of V8, TypeScript, Vue.js, Jest, React, Angular, Aurelia and Ember came out. We're not linking them all again 🤣

Please note, this list is just a selection and isn't *exhaustive*.. you've got [our issue archive](https://javascriptweekly.com/issues) for that :-)

## 📘 Top tutorials of 2018

[Going Beyond `console.log()`](https://medium.com/@mattburgess/beyond-console-log-2400fdf4a9d8) — While `console.log()` may form the basis of many people’s debugging strategies, the console object has a *lot* more to offer, as covered here. This was our top tutorial of the year with over 12,000 of you clicking.

Matt Burgess

[33 Concepts Every JavaScript Developer Should Know](https://github.com/leonardomso/33-js-concepts#readme) — A curated collection of links to tutorials on 33 different areas of JavaScript it’s worth understanding well, and not only one of our most popular links but one of the most starred projects on GitHub too.

Leonardo Maldonado
