## Top Stories

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/cpress/image/upload/w_1280,e_sharpen:60,q_auto/q5enb5o55iwrllzr5r42.jpg)

🎵 [Strudel REPL: Live JavaScript Music in the Browser](https://strudel.tidalcycles.org/) — This is a lot of fun. It’s a little online sandbox for putting together small musical experiments written in JavaScript. Use the ‘shuffle’ button at the top right until you find something you like the sound of. There’s [a tutorial](https://strudel.tidalcycles.org/learn/getting-started) on building your own, too. If you make any good ones, send them in to us and we might link to them.

Felix Roos et al.

[The ECMAScript 2023 Language Specification](https://tc39.es/ecma262/) — It’s that time of the year again. The latest ECMAScript spec, which standardizes what we know as JavaScript to some extent, is now in draft. This is not bedtime reading, of course, but is a fundamental part of what makes the language tick.

ECMA International

💡 If you do decide to brave the spec, [this guide on 'how to read the ECMAScript specification'](https://timothygu.me/es-howto/) will get you on the right path.

## 👀 Elsewhere This Week

Did you know we have several other newsletters we publish each week? We thought we'd do a quick roundup of what's going on with each of them in case they're of interest to you:

**REACT:** In [this week's *React Status*](https://react.statuscode.com/issues/327) we wondered if 'React is the new IBM', got to build a word scrambling game, and detected unnecessarily mounted React components.

**POSTGRES:** In [this week's *Postgres Weekly*](https://postgresweekly.com/issues/494) we learnt how to create Mermaid diagrams from SQL, create type constraints at the database level, and found out what the real 'billion dollar mistake' is. It's not nulls!

**FRONTEND:** In [this week's *Frontend Focus*](https://frontendfoc.us/issues/581) we focused on iOS and iPadOS's forthcoming Web Push API support, creating accessible carousels, and a surprisingly easy way to create a 'dark mode' theme for your site.

**NODE.JS:** In [this week's *Node Weekly*](https://nodeweekly.com/issues/475) we discovered two new ways to make type-safe MongoDB queries, and found a way to have Node play endless clicker games on your behalf.

**RUBY:** In [this week's *Ruby Weekly*](https://rubyweekly.com/issues/642), a Rails core team member told us why we shouldn't indulge in *monkey patching*, we learnt to create an intelligent FAQ with GPT-3, and met the Rails Foundation's new executive director.

**JAMSTACK:** In [this week's *JAMstacked*](https://jamstack.email/issues/75), Brian Rinaldi pondered React's role in the whole Jam space, and shared a few useful tools with us.

**GO:** In [this week's *Go Weekly*](https://golangweekly.com/issues/449), we played a game of Solitaire on the terminal, discovered a Go-powered Web browser, and learnt more about what's new in Go 1.20.

**RUST:** Sorry, we don't have a Rust newsletter, but we get asked that a lot!
