[The Web Almanac 2022's Take on the State of JavaScript in the Real World](https://almanac.httparchive.org/en/2022/javascript) — What’s better: a survey, or real world usage data from 8 million sites? If you prefer the latter, this is for you. [*The Web Almanac*](https://almanac.httparchive.org/en/2022/) is an annual ‘state of the web’ report into what technologies are being used online and amongst the [over 20 chapters of insights](https://almanac.httparchive.org/en/2022/table-of-contents) is a JavaScript-specific chapter. Some highlights:

- More JavaScript is going over the wire than ever before. 1.3MB for a page only puts you at the 90th percentile(!) About half of it goes unused too..
- 77% of mobile pages have render-blocking scripts in `<head>`
- Dynamic `import` is barely seen – about 0.3-0.4% of pages.
- [Web Workers](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Web_Workers_API/Using_web_workers) are used on 12% of pages.
- Of the 1,000 top sites, 17% use webpack, ~1.5% for Parcel.
- *Psst.. jQuery is still used all over the place..*
