[Turbopack: A Rust-Powered 'Successor' to Webpack](https://vercel.com/blog/turbopack) — With over 3 billion downloads, [webpack](https://webpack.js.org/) is the current champion of the bundlers (though [Vite](https://vitejs.dev/) has been rapidly advancing). Vercel found it too slow and has funded work on [Turbopack](https://turbo.build/). It's a project to keep an eye on and boasts huge performance boosts over webpack or even Vite, **but there are some limitations to take into account:**

- It's arguable if it's a "successor" to webpack, as Turbopack can't do much of what Webpack can yet, and while they share a creator, webpack is an active project with maintainers of its own.
- It's *very* connected to React and Next.js 13 (also released this week – more on that next) – [Vue and Svelte support](https://turbo.build/pack/docs/features/frameworks#vue-and-svelte) is to follow.
- The similarly Rust-powered [SWC](https://swc.rs/) is used to transpile JS/TS.
- Vite's Evan You posted [🐦 a Twitter thread with his thoughts](https://twitter.com/youyuxi/status/1585040261922820096) on Turbopack so far.
- TLDR: Unless you're big into Next.js, stick with your existing tools.

[Next.js 13 Unveiled at Next.js Conf](https://nextjs.org/blog/next-13) — [Next.js Conf 22](https://nextjs.org/conf) took place earlier this week and as well as Turborepo (above), Vercel unveiled the latest version of the site framework that has become ['inextricably linked'](https://twitter.com/ascorbic/status/1585004156615479296) to React. It gains:

- Support for an `app/` directory to improve routing, [layouts](https://nextjs.org/blog/layouts-rfc), and Server Components support – can coexist with existing approaches.

- A [brand new font system](https://nextjs.org/blog/next-13#nextfont).
