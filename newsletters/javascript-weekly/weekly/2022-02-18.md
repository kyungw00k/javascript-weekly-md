## Top Stories

[The State of JS 2021 Results](https://2021.stateofjs.com/en-US/) — Despite saying 2021, the survey took place this January, so the results are up to date and represent the answers given by the 16,085 respondents. As with any survey or poll, be careful in how you interpret the results ([many](https://twitter.com/twerske/status/1494385789714649111) [folks](https://twitter.com/youyuxi/status/1493927355399606273) [have](https://twitter.com/meeroslav/status/1494254037197373441) [noted](https://twitter.com/aaronksaunders/status/1494447797797593090) [problems](https://twitter.com/mor10/status/1494385609770618892) around diversity or methodology) but nonetheless we always enjoy seeing the results from year to year and seeing what people have mentioned. Highlights:

- [esbuild](https://esbuild.github.io/) was the tool with the largest year on year satisfaction growth.
- 5.6% of respondents are [using Deno.](https://2021.stateofjs.com/en-US/other-tools/#runtimes)
- [Long standing libraries](https://2021.stateofjs.com/en-US/other-tools/#libraries) Axios, Lodash and Moment remain the most popular.
- *Syntax* is by far [the most popular podcast](https://2021.stateofjs.com/en-US/resources/#podcasts) but there are 24 others in the list to check out.
- TypeScript is by far the most common [alternative 'flavor' of JavaScript](https://2021.stateofjs.com/en-US/other-tools/#javascript_flavors), with Elm a distant second place.
- Shawn 'swyx' Wang has a neat [round up / conclusion post.](https://www.swyx.io/state-of-js-2021/)

GitHub has added a handy feature to its custom variant of Markdown so that you can [write diagrams directly in Markdown](https://github.blog/2022-02-14-include-diagrams-markdown-files-mermaid/) using the same format as [Mermaid.js](https://mermaid-js.github.io/mermaid/#/). This is a fantastic way to make your README files more interesting if you have processes that can be outlined by way of a diagram – so check it out.
