## 🎁 A few bonus items..

Just some things I've seen that didn't fit anywhere else, but I wanted to give a quick mention:

- 🤖 [Google has released Gemma 3n](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/introducing-gemma-3n-developer-guide/), its latest locally-runnable LLM. It performs very well for its size (5B and 8B parameter counts) and if you've got the right hardware, you can [run it easily with Ollama](https://ollama.com/library/gemma3n) and similar tools.
- Coder extraordinare *Bisqwit* is back from a long hiatus with [▶️ a video on cracking game passwords in 1980s NES games.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2xzWmTfapuM)
- David Heinemeier Hansson, of *Ruby on Rails* fame, has released [Omarchy](https://omarchy.org/) – an 'opinionated Arch/Hyprland setup' to get Linux working in an aesthetically pleasing way for software developers, whether using Ruby or not.
- A notably buff John Carmack recently gave a talk (complete with his first slide deck *ever*) about [▶️ his team's work and research directions](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3pdlTMdo7pY) around machine learning, multi-task learning, and getting AI to play Atari games.
