[Deno v. Oracle: Cancelling the JavaScript Trademark](https://deno.com/blog/deno-v-oracle) — Did you know Oracle formally owns the ‘JavaScript’ trademark? There have been a few efforts to change this over the years but Oracle isn’t listening, so the Deno team formally filed a petition to cancel the trademark.

Deno

⚖️ And, as of this week, [it looks like Oracle is lawyering up to defend it **[PDF]**.](https://deno.com/blog/deno-v-oracle/20241204-notice-of-appearance.pdf)

🎂 [JavaScript Turns 29 Years Old](https://web.archive.org/web/20070916144913/http://wp.netscape.com/newsref/pr/newsrelease67.html) — If you’re wondering *why* Oracle has the JavaScript trademark, it's because Sun owned the name and [Oracle acquired Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acquisition_of_Sun_Microsystems_by_Oracle_Corporation) (which is also why it owns Java). As someone who was posting inane questions in `comp.lang.javascript` in 1996, this news makes me feel old – no-one could have anticipated what JavaScript would be today.
