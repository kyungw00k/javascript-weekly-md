![](https://res.cloudinary.com/cpress/image/upload/w_1280,e_sharpen:60,q_auto/qhmdk7uzmxbk6yntbapd.jpg)

[The ECMAScript Records and Tuples Proposal Has Been Withdrawn](https://github.com/tc39/proposal-record-tuple/issues/394) — Several years in the making, the [record and tuples proposal](https://github.com/tc39/proposal-record-tuple) offered two new deeply immutable data structures to JavaScript, but at this week’s TC39 meeting, the consensus was to drop it.

There have, however, been some more positive updates:

- [A proposal for introducing enums to JavaScript](https://github.com/tc39/proposal-enum) has advanced to stage 1. It seeks the adoption of a compatible form of TypeScript's `enum` declaration. [Slidedeck here.](https://onedrive.live.com/personal/934f1675ed4c1638/_layouts/15/Doc.aspx?sourcedoc=%7Be9bda98c-42e0-4c88-946e-76c35aac796f%7D)
- The [Deferred re-exports proposal](https://github.com/tc39/proposal-deferred-reexports) has advanced to stage 2. There's [a slidedeck walking through the feature.](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1ats5CbsgalobhnfFIR2b1QAdaLRe4yVI55meo_ARqdU/edit#slide=id.p)
- The [Upsert](https://github.com/tc39/proposal-upsert) and [Composites](https://github.com/tc39/proposal-composites) proposals have been advanced.

It's worth [following Rob Palmer](https://bsky.app/profile/robpalmer.bsky.social) if you want to keep up to date with TC39 goings-on as he's always sharing the latest news.

**IN BRIEF:**

- [Mozilla has enabled its Temporal implementation by default](https://spidermonkey.dev/blog/2025/04/11/shipping-temporal.html) as of Firefox 139.
- 📘 Dr. Axel Rauschmayer has [released *Exploring TypeScript: TS 5.8 edition*](https://exploringjs.com/ts/), a book bringing together all of his recent research into modern TypeScript development. You can buy the book but also [read it all online for free](https://exploringjs.com/ts/book/index.html) in HTML form, if you prefer.
- [ESLint has added support for 'bulk suppressions'](https://eslint.org/blog/2025/04/introducing-bulk-suppressions/), a way to make adopting stricter linting rules more manageable.
- Dan Abramov is back with [JSX Over The Wire](https://overreacted.io/jsx-over-the-wire/), a fantastic meditation on the evolution of passing data and behavior between servers and clients and how things could get even better.
- 🤖 Microsoft's Burke Holland [▶️ shows off VS Code's new 'agent mode' feature](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dutyOc_cAEU) which really makes Copilot far more powerful and a fine alternative to dedicated AI-powered editors. I've been using it a lot this week.
- [Google has made Firebase App Hosting generally available.](https://firebase.blog/posts/2025/04/apphosting-general-availability) It offers a streamlined, fully-managed deployment process for Angular and Next.js apps.
- Sacha Greif, the name behind the State of JavaScript survey, has [just launched](https://dev.to/sachagreif/launching-the-first-ever-state-of-devs-survey-23il) the first 'State of Devs' survey. 🗳️ [You can fill it out here](https://survey.devographics.com/en-US/survey/state-of-devs/2025?source=javascriptweekly).
